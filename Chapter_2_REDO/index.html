Energies of Subatomic Particles

Before we begin our journey looking at how semiconductor devices work, we need to understand the inner workings of atoms. The Hydrogen Atom, due to its simple structure, has been extensively studied, and provides a good starting point to understand the fundamentals, before we move onto semiconductors such as Silicon.

The Subatomic Particles

Each atom of any element is composed of certain subatomic particles. These subatomic particles are the electron, the proton and the neutron.

An electron is a negative charge carrier, with negligible mass. Each element has as many electrons as its atomic number. A proton is a positive charge carrier, and contributes to the mass of the atom. Together, these two subatomic particles ensure that the atom is neutral, or has a net charge of 0.

A hydrogen atom has 1 proton and 1 electron, but no neutron.
