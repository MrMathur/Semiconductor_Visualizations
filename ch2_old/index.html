The Subatomic Particles

Once again, we look at the simplistic case of a Hydrogen atom, that consists of one electron and one proton.

These subatomic particles are equally, but oppositely, charged.

Coulomb Force of Attraction

Due to these charges, there is a force of attraction that acts on these particles.

This force is governed by the Coulomb law, which states that \[F = {-k * q1 * q2 \over r^2}\]

Here k is a constant, q1 and q2 are the charges of both particles, and r is the distance between the two.

The Potential Energy

If we consider the two charges as a system, the coulombic force gives rise to a potential energy. This energy is governed by the equation \[E = {-k * q1 * q2 \over r}\]

As with any potential, it is defined as 0 at a reference point.

The potential energy of all other points is defined as the negative of the work done by an external force, in moving the charge from that point to the reference point.

Total Energy of Electron

The classical model assumed that the electron revolves around the proton, giving rise to a kinetic energy defined by \[E = {1* m * v^2\over 2\ }\]
